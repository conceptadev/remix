01 · ownership

Why this exists

The old surface layer mixed ordinary box decoration with the special rendering needed for CSS-shaped effects. The refactor makes the exception explicit and lets the normal case remain normal.

Before

One layer owned too much

RemixSurfaceLayerSpec carried color, radius, blur, outline, gradients, and shadows. Components exposed paired surface/overlay fields through public wrapper widgets.

  • Two places could own ordinary fill and geometry.
  • Radius could drift from the Mix box.
  • Every component exposed renderer plumbing.
  • Normal decoration paid an abstraction cost.
After

Advanced paint becomes an augmentation

BoxSpec remains authoritative. RemixSurfaceEffectsSpec carries only background/foreground layers, backdrop blur, and an offset outline.

  • Empty effects use real Mix widgets.
  • One aggregate replaces paired fields.
  • Private adapters preserve layout and semantics.
  • Fortal keeps multi-layer CSS fidelity.

07 · invariants

Constraints are the contract

The private adapters exist to preserve normal Mix behavior. Their limitations are explicit, testable, and more useful than a wrapper that accepts ambiguous geometry.

  • Effects require a rectangular BoxDecoration.
  • ShapeDecoration and BoxShape.circle are unsupported.
  • An otherwise undecorated box needs a radius-only decoration.
  • Outline stroke alignment must be inside.
  • Gradient insets are empty or match gradient count.
  • Insets must be finite and non-negative.
  • Shadow values and a shadow token are mutually exclusive.
  • Paint never owns hit testing or semantics.
  • Clips contain blur/inner paint, not outer paint.
  • Empty effects keep the real Mix fast path.
  • Nullable effects interpolate instead of snapping.
  • Blend bounds expand for paint, then stop at descendant clips.

08 · extension guide

Where should new paint live?

Follow these decisions in order. Most new visuals should stop at the first question.

  1. Can Mix BoxDecoration express it?
    Put it in the component container spec.
  2. Does it need multi-gradients, inset shadows, blur, offset outline, or foreground paint?
    Use an effects aggregate.
  3. Does the component have independently painted anatomy?
    Name one effects slot per role.
  4. Does transient state augment the base?
    Merge aggregates and verify interpolation.
  5. Can paint overflow?
    Verify custom paint bounds and subtree blending.
